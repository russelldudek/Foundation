Foundation Data is asking this role to do something more specific than “run operations.” The real assignment is to make strategy executable across Scout, tagging, analytics, marketing, and AI while preserving the speed of a small company and forcing time toward revenue, repeatability, and gross profit.

That is the kind of operating problem I have spent my career solving. At Vape-Jet, I lead AI-first operating transformation across production, quality, purchasing, customer success, support, engineering issue flow, ERP/Odoo, telemetry, and AI-assisted workflows. At Amazon, I built the routines, labor planning, throughput visibility, standard work, and escalation mechanisms required to operate a 24/7 customer-backward system supporting approximately five million second-day orders annually. Earlier, at Compunetics, I connected engineering, manufacturing, quality, customer delivery, and high-reliability technical programs through measurable process controls.

I also work directly in AI-enabled workflow design. Through DudeWorth, I develop opportunity frameworks and agentic patterns that connect business value, data readiness, human authority, evaluation, governance, adoption burden, and time-to-value. My bias is not to decorate a process with AI. It is to redesign the work, define where people retain authority, instrument the result, and redeploy the capacity that is actually released.

What makes the opportunity especially compelling is the symmetry between Foundation Data’s customer promise and its internal operating challenge. The company helps automotive leaders convert fragmented data into trusted priorities; this role must do the same with a growing portfolio of product, tagging, analytics, marketing, and AI work. I would treat operational clarity as part of the product strategy: every initiative should expose the decision it improves, the economic load it carries, the people whose commitment it requires, and the evidence that changes the next allocation decision.

For Foundation Data, I would begin with a lightweight “Operating Load Path”: each priority becomes an explicit initiative contract linking the customer decision, economic logic, accountable owner, dependencies, capacity load, trust requirements, and leading evidence. A weekly load review would then give leadership a disciplined way to accelerate, sequence, repair, or stop work. That mechanism is designed to free the COO from carrying cross-functional context personally while keeping the company direct and pragmatic.

I have built a role-specific candidate vision, 120-day entry plan, and weekly operating review to make this approach concrete. The package is included with this application, and the complete candidate vision is available at https://russelldudek.github.io/Foundation/.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss which decisions you most want this role to absorb, where Scout and tagging currently encounter hidden operating load, and how Foundation Data can scale its own execution with the same clarity it delivers to customers.
