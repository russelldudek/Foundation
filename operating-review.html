Weekly Operating Mechanism

Initiative Load Review

A 45-minute exception-and-decision cadence for Scout, tagging, analytics, marketing, partnerships, and internal AI.
Purpose: convert status into decisions. Only changed evidence, aged blockers, cross-functional dependencies, forecast variance, and resource trade-offs earn synchronous meeting time.

Meeting Inputs - Due Before the Review

  • Updated initiative contract and changed evidence.
  • Blockers older than the agreed escalation threshold.
  • Forecast variance and the assumption that changed.
  • Capacity or delivery-load changes that affect economics.
  • A specific decision request when leadership attention is required.

Initiative Contract

One complete record per strategic initiative

1. Decision the initiative improves

What customer or company decision becomes faster, clearer, more accurate, or more actionable?

2. Economic logic

Revenue, gross profit, retention, capacity, risk, or infrastructure leverage. State the unit and baseline; do not invent a target.

3. Accountable owner and decision rights

One owner. Name required commitments from Data, Analytics, Product, Marketing, Revenue, Finance, Operations, or external partners.

4. Capacity and dependency load

Engineering effort, analytics effort, tagging/QA load, client-delivery burden, support load, vendor dependency, and expected timing.

5. Trust and adoption conditions

Data quality, evaluation, privacy/security, human authority, exception handling, behavior change, support ownership, and fallback.

6. Leading evidence

Cycle time, first-pass completeness, rework, adoption, usefulness, exception rate, support burden, reuse, delivery effort, forecast variance, or another defined signal.

7. Current posture and next decision

Accelerate / sequence / repair / stop. Include the reason, the evidence required, the owner, and the next decision date.

Meeting Hygiene

  • No status narration that can be read asynchronously.
  • No metric without a definition, owner, source, unit, baseline, cadence, and decision use.
  • No blocker without an owner, next action, and escalation date.
  • No AI pilot without a process owner, human authority, evaluation, adoption plan, and capacity-redeployment hypothesis.
  • No initiative remains green merely because activity continues; evidence and decision readiness define health.
