Initiative Contract
One page per strategic initiative

1. Decision the initiative improves

What specific customer or company decision becomes faster, clearer, more accurate, or more actionable?

2. Economic load

Revenue, margin, retention, capacity, risk, or infrastructure leverage. State the unit and baseline; do not invent a target.

3. Accountable owner and decision rights

One owner. Name required commitments from Data, Analytics, Product, Marketing, Revenue, Finance, or Operations.

4. Capacity and dependency load

Engineering effort, analytics effort, tagging/QA load, client-delivery burden, support load, and external/vendor dependency.

5. Trust conditions

Data quality, evaluation, privacy/security, human authority, exception handling, adoption ownership, and rollback/fallback where relevant.

6. Leading evidence

Signals that change the decision: cycle time, first-pass completeness, rework, adoption, usefulness, exception rate, support burden, reuse, delivery effort, forecast variance.

7. Current posture

Accelerate • Sequence • Repair • Stop. Include the reason and the next decision date.

Meeting Hygiene

  • No status narration that can be read asynchronously.
  • No metric without a definition, owner, unit, baseline, and decision use.
  • No blocker without an owner, next action, and escalation date.
  • No AI pilot without a process owner, human authority, evaluation, adoption plan, and capacity-redeployment hypothesis.
