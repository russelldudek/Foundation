Operating Model
The Operating Load Path
A minimum viable operating system for initiative economics, ownership, dependencies, capacity, and evidence.

Five-Part Initiative Contract

Contract clauseQuestionReview output
DecisionWhat customer or company decision must improve?Clear use case and decision owner
Economic loadWhat revenue, margin, retention, capacity, or risk does this carry?Explicit economic hypothesis and measure
OwnershipWho is accountable, and which functions must commit?One owner, named dependencies, decision rights
Trust loadWhat data quality, human authority, security, and adoption conditions must hold?Release/evaluation boundary and exception path
Evidence cadenceWhat leading evidence changes the decision?Accelerate, sequence, repair, or stop

Decision Architecture

What gets accelerated

Initiatives with clear customer decision value, economic leverage, credible ownership, and manageable trust/capacity load.

What gets sequenced

Important work whose dependency or capacity load would otherwise create hidden rework or roadmap thrash.

What gets repaired

Work with a strong use case but weak data definition, handoff, adoption owner, or operating control.

What gets stopped

One-off work that cannot justify its delivery load, lacks a decision owner, or fails to produce learning.

Balanced Scorecard

Initiative throughput • blocker age • tagging first-pass completeness and rework • Scout adoption/usefulness • gross-profit contribution • capacity released and redeployed • forecast reliability • learning velocity.
